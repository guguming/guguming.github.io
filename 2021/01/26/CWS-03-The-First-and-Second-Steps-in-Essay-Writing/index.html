3. The First and Second Steps in Essay Writing

发表于 2021-01-26 分类于 English
本文字数： 3.6k 阅读时长 ≈ 3 分钟

Four Steps in Writing an Effective Essay:

  1. Begin with a point or thesis.
  2. Support the thesis with specific evidence.
  3. Organize and connect the specific evidence.
  4. Write clear, error-free sentences.

Step 1: Begin with a Point, or Thesis

Discover what point you want to make and write that point out as a single sentence. This will tell you whether you have a clear and workable thesis. Also, you can use the thesis as a guide while writing your essay.

Understanding Thesis Statements

A good thesis statement does two things.

  1. First, it tells readers an essay’s topic.
  2. Second, it presents the writer’s attitude, opinion, idea, or point about that topic.

For example, look at the following thesis statement:

Owning a pet has several important benefits.

In this thesis statement, the topic is owning a pet; the writer’s main point is that owning a pet has several important benefits.

Writing a Good Thesis

To start, you need a topic that is neither too broad nor too narrow. Sometimes a subject must go through several stages of limiting before it is narrow enough to write about.

How to Limit and Focus Your Thesis

  • By clustering or making a list, you can find the topic that you know most about, that you are most interested in, or that you are most passionate about.
  • Several different thesis statements can be written about the same topic.
  • Your purpose will help determine your main point and may even help shape the organization of your paper.

1 Write Statements, Not Announcements

A thesis statement must make a point about a limited subject, rather than announcements. Inappropriate examples are as follows:

  • The subject of this paper will be my parents.
  • I want to talk about the crime wave in our country.
  • The baby-boom generation is the concern of this essay.

2 Avoid Statements That Are Too Broad

Remember, your thesis statement should be focused enough that it can be effectively supported in a five-paragraph essay. Inappropriate examples are as follows:

  • Disease has changed human history.
  • Insects are fascinating creatures.
  • Men and women are very different.

3 Avoid Statements That Are Too Narrow

Here are three statements that are too narrow:

  • The speed limit near my home is 65 miles per hour.
  • A hurricane hit southern Florida last summer.
  • A person must be at least 35 years old to be elected president of the United States.

The three statements are all simple facts that does not require any support. Such statements are sometimes called dead-end statements: there is no place to go with it. Remember, a thesis statement must be broad enough to require support in an essay.

4 Make Sure Statements Develop Only One Idea

Here are three statements that contain more than one idea:

  • One of the most serious problems affecting young people today is bullying, and it is time more kids learned the value of helping others.
  • Studying with others has several benefits, but it also has drawbacks and can be difficult to schedule.
  • Teachers have played an important role in my life, but they were not as important as my parents.

Remember, the point of an essay is to communicate a single main idea to readers. To be as clear as possible, then, try to limit your thesis statement to the single key idea you want your readers to know.

Step 2: Support the Thesis with Specific Evidence

When you are writing an essay, it will help if you write down an informal outline with three points that will support the thesis.

The Importance of Specific Details

Each supporting point must be developed with specific detail.

Specific details are valuable in two key ways. First, details excite the reader’s interest. They make writing a pleasure to read, for we all enjoy learning particulars about people, places, and things. Second, details serve to explain a writer’s points. They give the evidence needed for us to see and understand general ideas. An effective paragraph describes the details in a specific and lively way, while an ineffective paragraph asks us to guess about the details.

The Importance of Adequate Details

You must provide enough specific details to fully support the point in a body paragraph of an essay. Students may try to disguise unsupported paragraph through repetition and generalities. Do not fall into this “wordiness trap.” Be prepared to do the plain hard work needed to ensure that each paragraph has solid support.
